# Code Of Conduct

DevFlow is for people who want AI-assisted frontend work to become more careful, inspectable, and useful. The project should feel welcoming to contributors with different backgrounds, tools, and levels of AI experience.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and direct.
- Assume good intent while still naming technical risks clearly.
- Keep feedback focused on the work, not the person.
- Make room for questions, especially around safety, verification, and maintainability.
- Prefer concrete examples over vague criticism.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or conduct.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Pressuring contributors to share secrets, proprietary code, or paid provider credentials.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions after maintainers ask to refocus.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may moderate issues, discussions, pull requests, or community spaces to keep the project healthy. Depending on severity, this may include warnings, comment removal, temporary restrictions, or banning a participant from project spaces.

To report a conduct issue, contact the maintainers privately. Reports will be handled with care and as much confidentiality as practical.
